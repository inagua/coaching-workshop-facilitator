# Retrospective Techniques

## Purpose

Retrospectives help teams reflect on past experiences and identify improvements for the future.

Unlike post-mortems, which focus on a specific incident, retrospectives focus on:

- Team effectiveness
- Collaboration
- Processes
- Tools
- Communication
- Continuous improvement

The objective is to learn and improve.

Not to blame.

---

# Choosing the Right Retrospective

Different situations require different approaches.

| Situation | Recommended Technique |
|------------|------------|
| General team improvement | Start / Stop / Continue |
| Emotional events | Mad / Sad / Glad |
| Complex challenges | Sailboat |
| Learning-focused workshops | 4Ls |
| Collaboration issues | Remember the Future |
| Large amount of feedback | Lean Coffee |
| Recent incident | What Went Well / What Went Poorly |

---

# 1. Start / Stop / Continue

## Purpose

A simple and effective retrospective for identifying:

- New behaviors to introduce
- Existing behaviors to stop
- Positive behaviors to keep

---

## Questions

### Start

What should we start doing?

Examples:

- Earlier stakeholder involvement
- Better documentation
- Regular architecture reviews

### Stop

What should we stop doing?

Examples:

- Last-minute decisions
- Long email chains
- Duplicate meetings

### Continue

What is already working well?

Examples:

- Team collaboration
- Cross-team support
- Knowledge sharing

---

## Duration

15-30 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Regular retrospectives

✅ New teams

✅ Quick improvement workshops

---

# 2. Mad / Sad / Glad

## Purpose

Explore experiences through emotions.

This helps reveal things that a more analytical format may miss.

---

## Questions

### Mad

What frustrated us?

Examples:

- Delays
- Rework
- Unclear decisions

### Sad

What disappointed us?

Examples:

- Missed opportunities
- Lack of involvement
- Unused ideas

### Glad

What made us proud?

Examples:

- Team effort
- Support from others
- Successful outcomes

---

## Duration

20-40 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Team tensions

✅ Difficult periods

✅ Organizational change

---

## Facilitator Note

Focus on the event or situation.

Avoid discussions about individuals.

---

# 3. Sailboat

## Purpose

Visualize factors helping or slowing progress.

---

## Setup

Draw:

```text
       ☀ Goal

         ↑

   ⛵ Team

⚓ Anchors

💨 Wind

🪨 Risks
```

---

## Questions

### Wind

What helps us move forward?

Examples:

- Strong expertise
- Automation
- Good communication

---

### Anchors

What slows us down?

Examples:

- Technical debt
- Waiting for answers
- Process complexity

---

### Rocks

What risks do we see ahead?

Examples:

- Missing resources
- Dependency on suppliers
- Upcoming deadlines

---

### Destination

What destination are we trying to reach?

Examples:

- Better collaboration
- Successful delivery
- Team autonomy

---

## Duration

30-60 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Long-running projects

✅ Transformation initiatives

✅ Collaboration workshops

---

# 4. 4Ls

## Purpose

Capture balanced learning.

The 4Ls framework emphasizes continuous improvement.

---

## Questions

### Liked

What did we enjoy?

Examples:

- Effective workshops
- Helpful reviews
- Team support

---

### Learned

What did we learn?

Examples:

- New technical knowledge
- Organizational insights
- Better practices

---

### Lacked

What was missing?

Examples:

- Documentation
- Stakeholder involvement
- Feedback

---

### Longed For

What would we have liked?

Examples:

- Faster decisions
- Better tooling
- Clear priorities

---

## Duration

20-45 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Lessons Learned workshops

✅ End-of-project reviews

✅ Community retrospectives

---

# 5. Lean Coffee

## Purpose

A participant-driven retrospective.

The group decides which topics to discuss.

---

## Process

### Step 1

Participants propose topics.

Examples:

- Architecture reviews
- Test strategy
- Communication

---

### Step 2

Participants vote.

---

### Step 3

Discuss highest-priority topics first.

---

### Step 4

Continue until time expires.

---

## Duration

30-90 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Experienced teams

✅ Large groups

✅ Diverse topics

---

# 6. What Went Well / What Went Poorly

## Purpose

Simple analysis of successes and challenges.

Often used after:

- Incidents
- Releases
- Milestones

---

## Questions

### What Went Well?

Examples:

- Fast detection
- Good collaboration
- Effective communication

---

### What Went Poorly?

Examples:

- Missing tests
- Late reviews
- Incorrect assumptions

---

### Improvement Opportunities

What should change?

Examples:

- New processes
- Updated documentation
- Automation

---

## Duration

20-45 minutes

---

## Best Used For

✅ Post-Mortems

✅ Release retrospectives

✅ Lessons Learned workshops

---

# Selecting the Right Technique

## Incident Analysis

Recommended:

```text
Timeline
↓
What Went Well / Poorly
↓
Five Whys
↓
Actions
```

---

## Collaboration Improvement

Recommended:

```text
Remember the Future
↓
Theme Clustering
↓
Working Agreement
↓
Actions
```

---

## Team Improvement

Recommended:

```text
Start / Stop / Continue
↓
Prioritization
↓
Actions
```

---

## Strategic Reflection

Recommended:

```text
Sailboat
↓
Risk Analysis
↓
Improvement Plan
```

# Facilitator Guidelines

## Create Psychological Safety

Encourage:

- Respect
- Transparency
- Constructive feedback

Discourage:

- Personal criticism
- Defensiveness
- Blame

---

## Equal Participation

Ensure all voices are heard.

Consider:

- Silent brainstorming
- Round-robin sharing
- Dot voting

---

## Convert Insights Into Actions

Every retrospective should answer:

> What are we going to do differently?

Without actions, retrospectives quickly lose value.

---

# Common Anti-Patterns

## Complaint Session

Bad:

```text
Everything is broken.
```

Good:

```text
What can we improve?
```

---

## Blame Hunting

Bad:

```text
Who caused this?
```

Good:

```text
What enabled this problem?
```

---

## Too Many Actions

Bad:

```text
27 actions
```

Good:

```text
3-5 meaningful actions
```

Focus on impact over quantity.

# Retrospective Workflow Cheat Sheet

```text
Reflect
↓
Share
↓
Cluster
↓
Prioritize
↓
Define Actions
↓
Assign Owners
↓
Follow Up
```

Remember:

> A retrospective is successful not when people talk.
>
> A retrospective is successful when people leave with a better understanding and a commitment to improve.
