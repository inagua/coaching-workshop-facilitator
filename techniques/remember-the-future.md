# Remember the Future

## Purpose

Help participants move beyond current frustrations and imagine a future where the problem has already been solved.

This activity shifts the discussion from:

> "What is wrong today?"

to

> "What does success look like, and what did we do to achieve it?"

The technique is particularly effective when:
- emotions are involved,
- participants have different perspectives,
- there is a risk of blame,
- the objective is collaboration improvement,
- the group needs to create a shared vision.

---

# Workshop Types

Recommended for:

- Collaboration Improvement Workshops
- Architecture Reviews
- Team Alignment Workshops
- Organizational Change Workshops
- Lessons Learned Workshops

Less suitable for:

- Detailed Incident Post-Mortems
- Root Cause Analysis sessions
- Technical Troubleshooting workshops

---

# Outcomes

Expected deliverables:

- Shared vision of success
- Improvement themes
- Candidate actions
- Working agreements
- Change roadmap

---

# Duration

Typical duration:

| Group Size | Duration |
|------------|------------|
| 5-8 people | 30-40 min |
| 9-15 people | 40-60 min |
| 15+ people | 60-90 min |

# Materials

Physical workshop:

- Sticky notes

# Instructions

## Step 1 - Introduce the exercise

Facilitator script:

> Imagine we are one year in the future.
>
> The collaboration between our teams has become a success.
>
> Projects run smoothly.
>
> Decisions are understood.
>
> Feedback is constructive.
>
> Stakeholders are satisfied.
>
> Looking back, what happened? What changed? What did we do differently?

Emphasize:

- There are no wrong answers.
- This is a visioning exercise.
- We are not discussing feasibility yet.

Duration: 5 minutes

---

## Step 2 - Individual Reflection

Ask participants to answer:

### Future Success

- What is working exceptionally well?
- What are people saying about our collaboration?
- What has become easy that is difficult today?

### New Behaviors

- What do we do differently?
- What meetings exist now?
- What decisions are made differently?

### Removed Frictions

- What problems disappeared?
- What delays no longer exist?
- What misunderstandings are gone?

Participants write one idea per sticky note.

Duration: 10 minutes

---

## Step 3 - Share Stories

Each participant briefly presents their future vision.

Facilitator listens for patterns.

Examples:

- Earlier involvement of developers
- Faster responses
- Better architecture reviews
- Clear ownership
- Better documentation
- Stronger communication

Duration: 10-20 minutes

---

## Step 4 - Cluster Ideas

Group similar notes together.

Possible categories:

### Communication

Examples:

- Faster answers
- Better feedback loops
- Regular syncs

### Governance

Examples:

- Clear responsibilities
- Escalation path
- Defined ownership

### Deliverables

Examples:

- Better documents
- Earlier drafts
- Review cycles

### Decision Making

Examples:

- Architecture decisions explained
- Traceability
- Shared understanding

Duration: 10-15 minutes

---

## Step 5 - Extract Themes

For each cluster ask:

> What changed to make this possible?

Capture:

- Processes
- Behaviors
- Tools
- Responsibilities

Duration: 10 minutes

---

## Step 6 - Define Actions

Ask:

> What can we start doing now to move toward this future?

Create actions with:

- Owner
- Due date
- Success criteria

Duration: 15-20 minutes

---

# Facilitator Tips

## Focus on systems

Prefer:

> What changed in the collaboration system?

Avoid:

> Who finally started doing their job?

---

## Encourage balanced participation

Ask silent participants first.

Avoid a few participants dominating the discussion.

---

## Keep people in the future

If participants start discussing current problems:

Instead of:

> Let's discuss what happened.

Ask:

> In the successful future, how do we handle this situation?

---

## Extract behaviors

Many visions remain vague.

Example:

### Weak

> Communication is better.

### Strong

> Architecture reviews are scheduled within 5 working days and feedback is documented in a shared location.

---

# Example

## Context

TRACE and Architecture have experienced collaboration difficulties.

---

## Question

> Imagine it is July 2027.
>
> TRACE and Architecture are recognized as an example of effective collaboration.
>
> What changed?

---

## Sample Outputs

### Collaboration

- Developers are involved before architectural decisions are finalized.
- Architecture reviews occur within agreed lead times.

### Documentation

- SADs are reviewed iteratively.
- Architecture decisions are documented and traceable.

### Communication

- Monthly architecture sync exists.
- Escalation path is clear.

### Governance

- Ownership of XSD updates is explicit.
- Decision makers are identified upfront.

---

# When to Use

Use when:

✅ The future is more important than the past.

✅ The workshop aims to improve collaboration.

✅ Participants are likely to become defensive.

✅ The group needs a shared vision.

Avoid when:

❌ Root cause analysis is the primary objective.

❌ Technical diagnosis is required.

❌ Regulatory investigation is required.
