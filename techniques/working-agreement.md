# Working Agreement

## Purpose

A Working Agreement defines how a group commits to collaborate going forward.

Unlike action items, which focus on specific improvements, a Working Agreement establishes shared expectations, behaviors, responsibilities, and ways of working.

The goal is not to define an ideal world.

The goal is to answer:

> How do we want to work together from now on?

A good Working Agreement is:

- Shared
- Explicit
- Practical
- Observable
- Actionable

---

# Workshop Types

Recommended for:

- Collaboration Improvement Workshops
- Lessons Learned Workshops
- Architecture Workshops
- Agile Team Workshops
- Cross-Team Alignment Workshops

Can also be useful after:

- Post-Mortems
- Organizational Changes
- Team Formation
- Major Project Reviews

---

# Outcomes

Expected deliverables:

- Shared collaboration rules
- Clarified expectations
- Defined communication model
- Agreed responsibilities
- Reduced future misunderstandings

---

# Duration

| Group Size | Duration |
|------------|------------|
| 5–8 people | 20–30 min |
| 9–15 people | 30–45 min |
| 15+ people | 45–60 min |

---

# When to Use

A Working Agreement is particularly useful when people complain about:

- Communication
- Delays
- Ownership
- Lack of transparency
- Poor collaboration
- Confusing responsibilities
- Slow decisions

These symptoms often indicate that expectations are not aligned.

---

# Preparation

Before creating a Working Agreement, participants should already have:

- Identified collaboration challenges
- Discussed current pain points
- Shared desired future state
- Prioritized improvement areas

For example, after:

```text
Timeline
↓
Lessons Learned
↓
Remember the Future
↓
Working Agreement
```

# Instructions

## Step 1 – Introduce the Exercise

Facilitator script:

> We have identified what success looks like.
>
> Now let's agree on how we will work together to achieve it.
>
> The objective is to define practical agreements that everyone can understand and follow.

Duration: 5 minutes

---

## Step 2 – Identify Categories

Typical categories:

- Communication
- Responsibilities
- Decisions
- Deliverables
- Reviews
- Escalation
- Meetings

Choose only the categories relevant to the workshop.

Duration: 5 minutes

---

## Step 3 – Propose Agreements

Ask:

> What would good collaboration look like in practice?

Capture proposals.

Examples:

### Weak

```text
Communicate better
```

### Better

```text
Questions receive an acknowledgement within 2 working days.
```

### Weak

```text
Deliver earlier
```

### Better

```text
Documents are shared for review before being considered final.
```

Duration: 15–20 minutes

---

## Step 4 – Refine

For each proposal ask:

### Is it clear?

Would two different people interpret it the same way?

### Is it observable?

Can we determine whether it happened?

### Is it realistic?

Can the group actually do it?

### Is it shared?

Does everyone agree?

Duration: 10–15 minutes

---

## Step 5 – Confirm

Review every agreement.

Ask:

> Can everyone commit to this?

Possible responses:

- Agree
- Agree with modification
- Do not agree

Adjust if necessary.

Duration: 10 minutes

# Recommended Categories

## Communication

Examples:

```text
Questions receive an acknowledgement
within 2 business days.
```

```text
Major decisions are communicated
to affected stakeholders.
```

```text
Feedback is given directly to the

```

---

## Responsibilities

Examples:

```text
Ownership is identified before work begins.
```

```text
Decision makers are explicitly named.
```

```text
Each deliverable has a single accountable owner.
```

---

Examples:

```text
Draft architecture documents are

```

```text

```

```text
Major changes include an impact analysis.
```

```text
Decisions and rationale are documented.
```

# Facilitator Tips

## Make Agreements Observable

Bad:

```text
Be more collaborative
```

Good:

```text
Involve implementation teams before
architecture decisions are finalized.
```

---

## Focus on Behaviors

Avoid:

```text
Architecture must improve
```

Prefer:

```text
Architecture reviews are completed within
an agreed lead time.
```

---

## Avoid One-Sided Agreements

Bad:

```text
TRACE must provide feedback faster.
```

Better:

```text
Both Architecture and TRACE review
deliverables within 5 working days.
```

The agreement should feel balanced.

---

## Keep the Number Limited

Target:

```text
5 to 10 agreements
```

Too many agreements are rarely remembered.

---

# Example

## TRACE – Architecture Collaboration

### Communication

- Questions receive an acknowledgement within 2 working days.
- Decisions impacting TRACE are communicated proactively.

### Responsibilities

- Ownership of XSD updates is explicitly defined.
- A responsible reviewer is assigned for each architecture deliverable.

### Deliverables

- SADs are reviewed iteratively before final validation.
- Architecture decisions include rationale and assumptions.

### Meetings

- Monthly architecture sync between TRACE and Architecture.
- Escalation meeting when major blockers exceed 10 working days.

### Decisions

- Significant decisions are documented in a shared decision log.

# Review Mechanism

A Working Agreement is useful only if it is reviewed.

Recommended:

### Monthly

Ask:

- Which agreements did we respect?
- Which agreements did we break?
- Which agreements need adaptation?

This turns the Working Agreement into a living collaboration tool.
