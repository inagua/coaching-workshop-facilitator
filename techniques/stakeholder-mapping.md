# Stakeholder Mapping

## Purpose

Stakeholder Mapping helps identify the individuals, teams, organizations, and external parties that influence or are affected by a project, initiative, decision, change, or incident.

The technique helps answer questions such as:

- Who should be involved?
- Who should be informed?
- Who has decision authority?
- Who may support or resist the initiative?
- Whose needs must be considered?

The goal is to make stakeholder management explicit rather than relying on assumptions.

# When to Use

Use Stakeholder Mapping when:

✅ The initiative spans multiple teams

✅ Responsibilities are unclear

✅ Multiple organizations are involved

✅ Decisions require alignment

✅ Past issues were caused by missing stakeholders

---

Gather relevant information:

- Organization charts
- Team structures
- Project documentation
- Supplier information
- Customer information
- Previous project lessons learned

# Instructions

## Step 1 - Define the Scope

Ask:

> What project, initiative, change, or problem are we analyzing?

Examples:

```text
New Darts / ASO Integration
```

```text
TRACE–Architecture Collaboration
```

```text
AMAN Post-Mortem
```

Duration: 3 minutes

---

## Step 2 - Identify Stakeholders

Ask:

> Who is affected?

> Who influences decisions?

> Who provides expertise?

> Who controls resources?

> Who could block success?

Write one stakeholder per sticky note.

Examples:

```text
Trace Developers
```

```text
System Architects
```

```text
ATCO Representatives
```

```text
Supplier
```

```text
Airport Customer
```

```text
Operations
```

Duration: 10-15 minutes

---

## Step 3 - Group Stakeholders

Typical groups:

### Internal Teams

Examples:

- Operations
- Architecture
- QA
- Security

### Leadership

Examples:

- Engineering Managers

- Department Heads

### Customers

Examples:

- Airports
- Airlines
- Operational Users

### Suppliers

Examples:

- SITA
- Frequentis
- External Integrators

Duration: 5 minutes

## Step 4 - Assess Influence

Ask:

> How much influence does this stakeholder have?

Possible scale:

```text
High
Medium
Low
```

Or:

```text
1
2
3
4
5
```

Examples:

| Stakeholder | Influence |
|------------|------------|
| Architecture Lead | High |
| Engineering Manager | High |
| Developer | Medium |
| End User | Medium |
| Supplier | High |

Duration: 10 minutes

---

## Step 5 - Assess Interest

Ask:

> How impacted is this stakeholder?

Examples:

| Stakeholder | Interest |
|------------|------------|
| TRACE Team | High |
| Architecture Team | High |
| Supplier | High |
| Executive Sponsor | Medium |
| Other Teams | Low |

Duration: 10 minutes

---

## Step 6 - Create Influence / Interest Matrix

```text
                HIGH INTEREST
                     ↑

MANAGE CLOSELY     |    COLLABORATE
                   |
-------------------+----------------→
                   |
KEEP SATISFIED     |    MONITOR
                   |
                     LOW INTEREST
```

---

### High Influence / High Interest

Manage closely.

Examples:

- Architecture Lead
- Engineering Manager
- Product Owner

---

### High Influence / Low Interest

Keep satisfied.

Examples:

- Sponsors
- Senior Leadership

---

### Low Influence / High Interest

Keep informed.

Examples:

- Developers
- Testers
- Operational Users

---

### Low Influence / Low Interest

Monitor.

Examples:

- Adjacent teams

# RACI Variant

Stakeholder Mapping can be extended into a RACI analysis.

Definitions:

| Role | Meaning |
|--------|--------|
| R | Responsible |
| A | Accountable |
| C | Consulted |
| I | Informed |

Example:

| Activity | TRACE | Architecture | Supplier |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Define Architecture | C | A/R | C |
| Implement | R | C | I |
| Review | C | A/R | I |
| Deploy | R | C | I |

# Facilitator Tips

## Look for Missing Stakeholders

Ask:

> Who is not in the room?

Many project issues originate from stakeholders who were not involved early enough.

---

## Don't Forget External Actors

Examples:

- Customers
- Suppliers
- Regulators
- Operations

Teams often focus only on their immediate environment.

---

## Challenge Assumptions

Ask:

> Are we sure this stakeholder has low influence?

Influence is often informal.

---

## Consider Future Stakeholders

Ask:

> Who will be impacted later?

Forward-looking initiatives often affect people who are currently absent.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Focusing Only on Hierarchy

Position does not always equal influence.

Example:

```text
A senior developer may influence
architecture decisions more than a manager.
```

---

## Ignoring Customers

Many internal workshops overlook external stakeholders.

Always ask:

> Who receives the outcome?

---

## Treating Teams as Single Stakeholders

A team may contain:

- Decision makers
- Contributors
- Reviewers

Separate them when necessary.
